## Core Response Principles

1. **Reply within 1 hour** — Response speed = trust
2. **Be warm but professional** — Not too casual, not too corporate
3. **Ask qualifying question** — Budget, occasion, timeline
4. **Always mention USP** — "Hand embroidered by skilled karigars"
5. **Be honest about timeline** — 15-25 days, no fake promises
6. **End with CTA** — "Send us your measurement" / "Share your design idea"

## Scenario 6: Quality / Authenticity Inquiry

**Customer asks:** "How do I know it's really hand embroidered?"

**Response:**
```
Namaste! 🙏

BEST question. Here's how to verify:

🔍 **Signs of REAL hand embroidery:**

1. **Slight irregularities** — Machine work is perfectly uniform. Hand embroidery has tiny variations that show the human touch.

2. **Thread texture** — Real zari work has a slightly raised texture you can feel with your fingers. Printing is flat.

3. **Back of the work** — Look at the back of the embroidery. You should see thread work, not just glue or print residue.

4. **Price honesty** — A fully hand-embroidered sherwani CANNOT be ₹999. Real karigar work at ₹80-150/hour adds up because 1 sherwani takes 100-300 hours.

5. **We show process** — We can send you photos/videos of your piece being embroidered. Our karigars work in Ahmedabad and we document everything.

**Our promise:**
Every LAQAB piece comes with a authenticity tag mentioning the karigar's name and hours spent on embroidery.

If you ever suspect it's not hand-embroidered, return it. No questions asked. We're that confident. 🙏

Kaif 🤵
LAQAB — Where Tradition Meets Tomorrow
```
